# Faire Seller Knowledge Base

> Comprehensive reference for wholesale selling on Faire. Built from Faire's official resources, support articles, and established wholesale best practices.

---

## 1. Faire Seller Best Practices

### Getting Started
- **Application quality matters.** Faire reviews product demand, brand establishment, product uniqueness, and overall brand presentation when considering applications. Invest time in your brand story and product photography before applying.
- **Complete your profile fully.** Brands with complete profiles (logo, banner, about section, policies) receive significantly more retailer engagement than incomplete ones.
- **Set realistic expectations.** New brands typically take 2-4 weeks to gain traction. Use this time to optimize listings and build your catalog.

### Core Success Factors
- **Consistency wins.** Brands that ship on time, maintain inventory accuracy, and respond to messages within 24 hours rank higher in Faire's algorithm.
- **Product-market fit.** Research what retailers in your category are looking for. Browse competing brands on Faire to understand pricing, presentation, and product assortment standards.
- **Seasonal readiness.** Have seasonal collections uploaded 6-8 weeks before the relevant buying season. Retailers plan ahead.
- **Faire Direct adoption.** Brands using Faire Direct to bring their own retailers onto the platform pay 0% commission on those orders, making it a critical growth lever.

### Communication Standards
- Respond to retailer messages within 24 hours (ideally same business day).
- Use Faire Messenger for all order-related communication to maintain a record.
- Be proactive about delays: notify retailers before a shipment deadline passes, not after.
- Unauthorized rebranding or repackaging of products is prohibited and requires written consent from the brand.

## 2. Product Listing Optimization

### Title Formatting
- **Structure:** [Brand Name] + [Product Name] + [Key Attribute] + [Size/Quantity if applicable]
- **Example:** "Handcrafted Ceramic Mug - Speckled Sage, 12oz"
- Keep titles under 80 characters for optimal display.
- Include the most-searched keyword naturally in the title.
- Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, or promotional language ("BEST SELLER!!!").

### Description Writing
- **First sentence:** What the product is and who it's for. This appears in search previews.
- **Second paragraph:** Materials, dimensions, unique selling points.
- **Third paragraph:** Care instructions, packaging details, what's included.
- Use bullet points for specifications (dimensions, weight, materials).
- Include keywords retailers search for, but write naturally -- no keyword stuffing.
- Mention gift-worthiness if applicable; retailers actively search for giftable items.

### Image Requirements
- **Primary image:** Clean white or light background, product centered, well-lit.
- **Minimum resolution:** 1000x1000 pixels; 2000x2000 recommended.
- **Image count:** 4-8 images per product minimum.
- **Required shots:**
  - Hero/front shot on clean background
  - Lifestyle/context shot showing product in use
  - Detail/close-up shot highlighting texture or craftsmanship
  - Scale reference shot (with hand or common object)
  - Packaging shot (if packaging is a selling point)
- **File format:** JPEG or PNG, under 10MB per image.
- **Consistency:** Maintain the same photography style across your entire catalog for a cohesive brand presentation.

### Tags and Categories
- Use all available tag slots -- they directly affect search visibility.
- Select the most specific subcategory available, not just the parent category.
- Include material tags (ceramic, cotton, leather, etc.).
- Add occasion tags (gift, wedding, holiday, everyday).
- Review top-performing competitors' tag strategies for inspiration.

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### Wholesale Markup Guidelines
- **Standard wholesale markup:** 2x-2.5x cost of goods (COGS). This means your wholesale price should be 2-2.5 times your production cost.
- **Suggested retail price (SRP):** Typically 2x-2.5x your wholesale price, giving retailers a healthy margin.
- **Keystone pricing:** The most common retail model is "keystone" -- doubling the wholesale price for retail. Price your wholesale accordingly.

### Faire Commission Structure
- Faire charges commission on orders from new retailers discovered through the Faire marketplace.
- Orders from retailers you bring to Faire via Faire Direct carry 0% commission.
- Payment processing fees apply to all orders.
- Understand the difference between "Faire marketplace" orders and "Faire Direct" orders when calculating margins.

### Minimum Order Quantities (MOQ)
- **Opening order minimum:** Set this high enough to be worthwhile but low enough to attract first-time buyers. $100-$150 is a common sweet spot for most categories.
- **Reorder minimum:** Set lower than opening order (typically 50-75% of opening) to encourage repeat purchases.
- **Per-product minimums:** Consider case packs or minimum quantities per SKU to simplify fulfillment.
- Brands with lower minimums tend to get more first orders but may have lower average order values.

### Faire Direct Pricing
- Offer exclusive Faire Direct terms to incentivize retailers to order through your link.
- Common Faire Direct incentives: free shipping on first order, lower minimums, or introductory discounts.
- This is your most profitable channel on Faire -- prioritize driving traffic here.

### Pricing Pitfalls
- Do not undercut your own DTC (direct-to-consumer) pricing. Retailers will notice and lose trust.
- Account for Faire's commission when setting prices; do not treat it as an afterthought.
- Review and adjust pricing quarterly based on COGS changes, competitor pricing, and sell-through rates.

## 4. Retailer Relationships

### Outreach Best Practices
- **Personalize every message.** Reference the retailer's store name, location, or product mix.
- **Lead with value.** Explain why your products would resonate with their customers, not just why your brand is great.
- **Keep it brief.** 3-5 sentences maximum for initial outreach. Include a link to your Faire shop.
- **Follow up once.** If no response in 7-10 days, send one follow-up. After that, move on.

### Faire Direct Program
- Faire Direct lets brands share a unique link with retailers they already know.
- Retailers who order through your Faire Direct link get Net 60 payment terms and free returns.
- You pay 0% commission on Faire Direct orders (versus standard marketplace commission).
- Share your Faire Direct link at trade shows, in email outreach, on your website, and in your wholesale line sheet.
- This program is the single most effective tool for maximizing profit on Faire.

### Building Long-Term Relationships
- Include a handwritten thank-you note or brand card in first orders.
- Follow up 2-3 weeks after delivery to ask how products are selling.
- Offer early access to new collections for repeat retailers.
- Share merchandising tips or display suggestions specific to your products.
- Use Net 60 terms strategically: retailers who pay on time and reorder are your most valuable accounts.

### Managing Net 60 Terms
- Retailers on Faire can apply for 60-day payment terms, letting them buy now and pay later.
- Brands get paid by Faire upfront (minus commission), so Net 60 does not affect your cash flow.
- Retailers must meet eligibility requirements including business verification.
- This is a major selling point for retailers considering your brand.

## 5. Order Fulfillment

### Shipping Best Practices
- **Ship within 3 business days** of receiving an order. This is critical for Faire's brand scoring.
- **Upload tracking numbers** as soon as shipments go out. Faire monitors this metric.
- **Use Faire's shipping labels** when possible for discounted rates and automatic tracking.
- Pack securely with appropriate void fill, especially for fragile items.
- Include a packing slip with order details and your brand information.

### Late Shipment Avoidance
- Late shipments directly impact your brand's visibility and ranking on Faire.
- If you cannot ship on time, contact the retailer immediately and update the expected ship date in Faire.
- Set realistic processing times in your shop settings -- it is better to overestimate and ship early.
- During high-volume periods (holiday season, after trade shows), temporarily extend your processing time.
- Monitor your "ship on time" rate in the brand portal; aim for 95%+ consistently.

### Inventory Management
- Keep Faire inventory synced with other sales channels to avoid overselling.
- Mark items as "out of stock" promptly rather than canceling orders after the fact.
- Use Faire's inventory management tools or integrate via API for real-time sync.
- Plan production runs around Faire's seasonal peaks (January for spring buying, July for holiday buying).

### Returns and Damage
- Respond to return/damage claims within 48 hours.
- Faire's return policy protects both brands and retailers; understand the specifics.
- Document packaging quality to dispute fraudulent damage claims if needed.
- Track return rates by product to identify quality or packaging issues.

## 6. Growth Strategies

### Seasonal Planning Calendar
| Month | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| January | Upload spring/summer collections; retailers buying for Q2 |
| February-March | Trade show season; heavy retailer prospecting |
| April-May | Summer collection push; outdoor/seasonal items |
| June-July | Upload fall/holiday collections; retailers buying for Q4 |
| August-September | Holiday prep; gift guides and holiday bundles |
| October | Black Friday/Cyber Monday prep; promotional planning |
| November-December | Fulfill holiday orders; plan next year's line |

### Marketing on Faire
- **Faire-funded promotions:** Participate in Faire's marketplace promotions and sales events when offered.
- **Brand page optimization:** Treat your Faire brand page like a storefront -- update the banner seasonally, keep the "About" section compelling.
- **Social proof:** Encourage satisfied retailers to leave reviews on Faire.
- **Email marketing:** Build a wholesale email list and drive traffic to your Faire Direct link.

### Brand Storytelling
- Retailers buy from brands they believe in. Your "About" section should tell a compelling origin story.
- Highlight what makes your products unique: materials, process, social impact, sustainability.
- Include founder photos and workshop/studio images to humanize your brand.
- Update your brand story to reflect growth, awards, press mentions, and milestones.

### Expanding Your Catalog
- Analyze your best sellers and create variations (new colors, sizes, scents).
- Launch curated collections or bundles for specific occasions.
- Introduce complementary products that encourage larger orders.
- Test new products with limited runs before committing to full production.

## 7. Faire Platform Tips

### Search Ranking Factors
Faire's algorithm considers multiple factors when ranking brands and products in search results:
- **Sell-through rate:** Products that retailers reorder frequently rank higher.
- **Ship-on-time rate:** Brands with 95%+ on-time shipping get priority.
- **Response time:** Quick message responses signal an engaged, reliable brand.
- **Profile completeness:** Fully completed profiles with all images and descriptions rank higher.
- **Review quality:** Positive retailer reviews boost visibility significantly.
- **Recency:** Recently updated products and newly added items get a temporary boost.

### SEO on Faire
- Use keywords retailers actually search for in your titles and descriptions.
- Research search terms by browsing Faire as a retailer and noting autocomplete suggestions.
- Category selection matters: being in the right category is more important than trying to appear in multiple.
- Update product titles and descriptions quarterly based on performance data.

### Faire Algorithm Insights
- Faire rewards consistency over spikes. Steady shipping, messaging, and catalog updates outperform sporadic activity.
- New brands get a "new brand boost" in search results for approximately 30 days. Maximize this period.
- Brands that use all available features (Faire Direct, promotions, collections) tend to rank higher.
- Canceled orders and late shipments have an outsized negative impact. One cancellation can undo weeks of positive activity.

### Brand Portal Navigation
- Check the "Insights" tab regularly for data on views, conversion, and retailer behavior.
- Use the "Retailers" tab to track outreach and follow up with interested shops.
- The "Orders" tab shows pending, shipped, and delivered orders -- monitor daily.
- Set up email notifications for new orders and messages to maintain fast response times.

## 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Listing Mistakes
- **Poor primary image.** The first image is your storefront. A dark, cluttered, or low-resolution primary image kills conversion.
- **Missing or vague descriptions.** "Beautiful handmade item" tells retailers nothing. Be specific about materials, dimensions, and use cases.
- **Incorrect categorization.** Being in the wrong category means the right retailers never find you.
- **Too few images.** Listings with fewer than 4 images convert at a fraction of the rate of those with 6+.

### Pricing Mistakes
- **Pricing too low.** Underpricing signals low quality and erodes margins. Price confidently based on your value proposition.
- **Ignoring Faire commission in margins.** Always calculate your net margin after Faire's commission.
- **Inconsistent pricing across channels.** Retailers will check your DTC pricing. Wholesale must be appropriately lower.
- **Setting MOQs too high.** High minimums scare off first-time buyers. Start lower and increase once you have traction.

### Fulfillment Mistakes
- **Slow shipping.** This is the number one brand killer on Faire. Ship within your stated processing time, every time.
- **No tracking numbers.** Always upload tracking. Untracked shipments hurt your metrics.
- **Poor packaging.** Damaged goods lead to returns, bad reviews, and lost retailers.
- **Overselling.** Canceling orders because you ran out of stock is worse than being out of stock. Keep inventory accurate.

### Relationship Mistakes
- **Generic outreach.** Copy-paste messages get ignored. Personalize every retailer contact.
- **Ignoring messages.** Slow responses signal unreliability. Check messages daily.
- **Not using Faire Direct.** Leaving commission on the table for retailers you already know is a significant revenue leak.
- **Disappearing after the sale.** Follow up, ask for feedback, offer support. Relationships drive reorders.

### Platform Mistakes
- **Neglecting your brand page.** An outdated banner or stale "About" section makes your brand look inactive.
- **Not participating in promotions.** Faire's marketplace events drive significant traffic. Opt in when available.
- **Ignoring analytics.** The brand portal insights tab shows you exactly what is and is not working. Review it weekly.
- **Inconsistent activity.** Faire rewards active brands. Log in regularly, update listings, and engage with the platform.
